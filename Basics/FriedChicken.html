Fried Chicken

Description

Golden, crispy, and irresistibly juicy, fried chicken is a comfort food classic enjoyed around the world. The secret lies in a well-seasoned coating and the perfect fry—hot enough to create a crunchy crust, but gentle enough to keep the meat tender and moist inside. Traditionally, chicken pieces are marinated in buttermilk to enhance flavor and tenderness, then dredged in a spiced flour mixture before being fried to perfection.

Whether served with mashed potatoes and gravy, tucked into a sandwich, or enjoyed cold the next day, fried chicken is beloved for its satisfying crunch and rich flavor. From Southern kitchens in the United States to international variations like Korean-style double-fried chicken, this dish is endlessly adaptable and always crowd-pleasing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (cut into pieces) or ~8 bone-in chicken thighs/drumsticks
  • 2 cups buttermilk (or 2 cups milk + 2 tbsp vinegar/lemon juice as substitute)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch (for extra crispiness)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for spice)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps:

  1. Marinate the Chicken
    • Place chicken pieces in a large bowl. Pour over the buttermilk and 1 teaspoon salt.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
  2. Prepare the coating
    • In another bowl, mix flour, cornstarch, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, black pepper, and remaining salt.
  3. Coat the Chicken
    • Remove chicken from buttermilk (let excess drip off).
    • Dredge each piece in the flour mixture, pressing so it sticks.
    • For extra crispy chicken, dip the floured piece back into buttermilk, then into flour again (double coating).
  4. Fry the Chicken
    • Heat oil in a deep pot or skillet to 170–175°C (340–350°F).
    • Carefully lower chicken pieces into the hot oil, a few at a time (don’t overcrowd).
    • Fry 12–15 minutes for drumsticks/thighs, 8–10 minutes for wings/breasts, until golden brown and internal temp reaches 75°C (165°F).
  5. Drain and Serve
    • Remove chicken and place on a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.
    • Serve hot with gravy, hot sauce, or your favorite dipping sauce.
