Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is the ultimate layered comfort dish, bringing together tender sheets of pasta, rich tomato sauce, creamy cheese, and savory meat in a baked casserole that’s both hearty and satisfying. Its origins trace back to Italy, where it has been celebrated as a family-style meal for generations, often prepared on Sundays or for special gatherings.

Each layer builds depth: pasta for structure, sauce for bold flavor, ricotta or béchamel for creaminess, and mozzarella for that irresistible golden, bubbling top. Whether made traditionally with beef and pork, or adapted with vegetables, seafood, or even plant-based proteins, lasagna remains a timeless dish that invites creativity while delivering warmth in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or pork/beef mix)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook lasagna noodles until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cook until soft. Add ground beef and cook until browned. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer 15–20 minutes./li>
  3. In a bowl, combine ricotta, egg, and half of the Parmesan. Stir well./li>
  4. In a greased 9×13 inch baking dish:
    • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
    • Add a layer of noodles.
    • Spread ricotta mixture over noodles.
    • Sprinkle mozzarella.
    • Repeat layers until everything is used, ending with sauce and a topping of mozzarella + Parmesan.
  5. Cover with foil (to prevent burning) and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 20–25 minutes, until cheese is bubbly and golden.
  6. Let lasagna cool 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm!
